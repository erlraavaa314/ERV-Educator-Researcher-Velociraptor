About Me

Hi, I'm Erlend!

I'm currently working as a lecturer at the Western Norway University of Applied Sciences (HVL) at campus Førde, which lies hidden away in the innermost depths of a beautiful fjord. (Yes, the view of the city looks excatly like the picture, don't google it).

I'm also writing up my PhD in computer science from UiB, where I specialized in algorithms and machine learning. I am particularly fascinated by the intersection of complexity theory and topology. My research focuses on finding optimal geometric representatives of global topological features of spaces.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I enjoy weightlifting, climbing, swimming, hiking, kayaking, skiing, boardgames, cooking, brewing, mushroom-hunting and reading, in addition to a variety of other activities that I have carefully curated in order to make me seem more interesting than I actually am.

Research Interests

Welcome to this section, where I go on an in-depth rant about my research interests! I'm passionate about exploring the intersection of computer science and mathematics to address complex challenges. My work revolves around two main areas: Parameterized Complexity Theory and Computational Topology.

Computer science

Algorithms

Complexity theory

Within Parameterized Complexity Theory, I focus on breaking down challenging computational problems into manageable components. By understanding the core challenges of these problems, I develop efficient algorithms for tasks like network analysis and data processing. Recently, I've been exploring the versatility of these algorithms across various domains, including biology and social networks. My objective is not only to address challenges but also to gain deeper insights and share my findings with the broader community.

Parameterized Algorithms and Complexity Theory

Topology

Computational Topology

Transitioning to Computational Topology, I investigate the exploration of hidden patterns and structures within complex data. Persistent Homology, in particular, serves as a powerful tool for uncovering these hidden gems, akin to solving a puzzle with data as the pieces. My research endeavors involve the development of efficient algorithms to implement Persistent Homology. By integrating concepts from Parameterized Complexity Theory and Computational Topology, I aim to enhance the accessibility and applicability of these methodologies in addressing real-world challenges. Let's embark on a journey to unravel the mysteries concealed within the data!
